Caffeine and sleep: why 6 hours matters

A common rule-of-thumb is to avoid substantial caffeine within ~6 hours of bedtime. For many people, going earlier works even better.

Why the “6 hours” rule exists

Even earlier caffeine can carry into bedtime. If falling asleep is hard, earlier timing is an easy variable to test.

When to be more conservative

If you’re sensitive, anxious, or prone to insomnia, try 8–10 hours as an experiment — and compare outcomes.
