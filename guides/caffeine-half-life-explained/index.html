Caffeine half-life explained

Half-life is a simple way to estimate how quickly caffeine declines. It’s not perfect — but it’s useful for planning cutoff time.

Based on research showing caffeine can affect sleep even 6 hours before bed
If you’re troubleshooting sleep, treat “6 hours” as a minimum starting point, then personalize with the calculators.

What half-life means

If your half-life is 5–6 hours, about half of the caffeine remains after that time. Longer half-life means caffeine lingers later into the evening.

Why it varies

Genetics, medications, pregnancy, and other factors can change how quickly you clear caffeine. That’s why sensitivity tiers exist.

How to pick a tier

If you’re unsure, start conservative (High sensitivity), then adjust after observing your sleep over a week.
